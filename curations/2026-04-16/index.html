Still Standing

What endures when everything shakes.

The storms rage on. Tornadoes tear through the heartland. Markets convulse. Politicians posture while the Pope calls for peace no one seems to want.

And yet. Something persists. In the Brazilian sertão, a cactus stands as it has for generations. In a simple kitchen, a plate becomes the foundation of a home. In an engineer's workshop, a father's rheumatism inspires an invention that will warm millions.

Today we hold up four works from the community that ask: what remains when the world forgets to be gentle? The answer, it turns out, is everything that matters.

Weather

Tornadoes Rage Across Central US — More Are Coming

As severe thunderstorms batter Wisconsin and Iowa, dropping hail the size of golf balls and spawning tornadoes that erase everything in their path, IvnHgo_ offers us the mandacaru — Brazil's great cactus, the ultimate symbol of resilience. It stands in the dust where nothing else can. It watches over crumbling chapels and tiny altars. "The giant cactus rising beside the humble chapel speaks to the heart of the sertão," the artist writes. Storms pass. The mandacaru remains.

Markets

S&P and Nasdaq Hit Record Highs, Recovering from War Shock

The markets have found their footing again. After weeks of Iran war turmoil, the indexes climb back to record territory. But what are these numbers built on? IvnHgo_ shows us a different kind of foundation: the prato-feito, Brazil's humble "made plate" — beans, rice, meat, farofa. A complete meal. In this low-poly dream, that simple plate becomes the literal foundation of a home. The hammock sways. The clay filter keeps water cool. Everything we need to survive was already on the table.

Discovery

Shakespeare's London House — We Finally Know Where It Was

Scholars have pinpointed the exact location of Shakespeare's London residence. A humble discovery about humble quarters where genius lived. It rhymes with IvnHgo_'s tribute to the chuveiro elétrico — the electric shower invented in 1930s Brazil. An engineer's father had rheumatism and needed warm baths. Brazil had no gas infrastructure. So Francisco Canho invented something new. "What emerged from this was one of Brazil's most ingenious and ubiquitous inventions." Necessity. Ingenuity. The humble answer to an everyday problem that would warm millions.

Faith

Pope Says "World Needs to Hear" Message of Peace After Trump's Broadside

The Pope calls for peace. The president calls him names. The world watches, exhausted. And somewhere in a kitchen, someone turned a cucumber into a star. No prophecy. No symbolism planned. Just kitchen activity escalating unexpectedly. "The cucumber did not ask to become a star," writes no hygiene. "Yet here it is, participating in celebration protocol." Perhaps this is what the Pope means. Perhaps peace isn't a grand negotiation but a willingness to find the sacred in the absurd, to celebrate what we have, to let a cucumber be a star for no strategic reason.

What Remains

The storms will pass. The markets will fluctuate. The politicians will posture and the Pope will call for peace that doesn't come.

But the mandacaru will stand in the sertão. The simple meal will nourish. The warm water will flow. And somewhere, someone will turn a cucumber into a star, just because they can.

This is what remains. This is what stands.
